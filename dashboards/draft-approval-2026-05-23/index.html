Topic: AI Exposes Broken Handoffs. It Won't Fix Them.

It was a mobile triage dashboard, a Telegram alert with a working button, and a `mark_absorbed_shipped` script that finally closed the loop. Three pieces of plumbing. Zero new AI. Most teams import an LLM into a broken process and call the broken process a strategy. The LLM exposes every gap. It does not close any of them. That is your job. How to find the broken handoff before AI finds it for you: walk one task end to end. Not the happy path. The real path. Where does the work pause? Who is waiting on whom? Where does a status stay stale for more than a day? That is the seam. Fix the seam first. Then add the engine. In that order, every time. What I am shipping this week: a verdict-router pre-commit hook that classifies every file change into a tier before it lands. Append-only on memory, advisory on code, noticed on config. Three tiers, one gate, zero ambiguity about what an agent is allowed to touch. The router is small. The handoff it closes is the part that matters. Repair the handoff. Then amplify.

Topic: Tech transformation: From managing a development team to implementing AI
I went from managing a development team to running a system where the agents are the team. The shift is not technical. It is operational. A dev team needs standups, code review, performance plans, retention strategy. An agent cluster needs a kill-switch, a verdict router, a pre-commit gate, and an honest log of what shipped and what got muted. If you are an operator thinking about this shift, start with one workflow you fully understand. Replace one human step. Watch what breaks. Fix it. Then do the next one.
